## About

I am an independent builder based in Wales. My work focuses on practical, real-world software systems rather than polished products or commercial platforms.

I am particularly interested in systems that must remain reliable under imperfect conditions, including missing data, hardware limits and operational failure. Much of my work is iterative and long-term, prioritising correctness, auditability and local control over convenience.

### Core areas

**Health data and modelling**
I build local-first systems for continuous glucose monitoring, metabolic analysis and personal health data. The long-term aim is personalised modelling that learns behavioural and physiological patterns over time while remaining transparent and auditable.

**Simulation and tooling**
I develop utilities around sim racing, telemetry and race preparation, focusing on tools that improve workflow rather than add complexity.

**Self-hosted systems**
I prefer systems that run under direct control rather than depending on external cloud services. Reliability, observability and failure handling are core design priorities.

**Welsh language and technology**
I am interested in improving the presence of Cymraeg within technical and computing spaces through translation and tooling.

### Approach

- Spec-first design where possible
- Reliability before features
- Explicit safety and failure handling
- Measured, iterative development
- Systems built for real use rather than demonstration
